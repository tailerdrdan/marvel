Marvel Studios

Marvel Studios, LLC[1] (originally known as Marvel Films from 1993 to 1996), also known as Marvel, is an American film studio based at The Walt Disney Studios, Burbank, California. It is a subsidiary of Walt Disney Studios, which is owned by the media conglomerate The Walt Disney Company. The president of the film studio is film producer Kevin Feige[2]. The studio was previously a subsidiary of Marvel Entertainment until The Walt Disney Company reorganized the companies in August 2015.

People who helped

The Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) is an American media franchise and shared universe centered on superhero films and other television series starring various superheroes produced by Marvel Studios, based on characters appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. A shared universe, much like the original Marvel Universe from the comics, was created by intersecting common plot elements, locations, casts, and characters. During the production of films and related media (such as Disney+ miniseries), several teams and organizations were formed, each with different goals and objectives.

Jack Kirby

Jack Kirby is an artist who, along with writers Stan Lee, Larry Lieber and fellow illustrators Don Heck and Steve Ditko, created the Marvel comics universe.

Larry Lieber

Larry Lieber (English Larry D. Lieber, born October 26, 1931, Manhattan, New York, USA)[1] is an American screenwriter and comic book artist, the younger brother of Stan Lee. One of the creators of such characters as Iron Man, Thor, Loki, Ant-Man.

Steve Ditko

Stephen Jay Ditko is a Czechoslovakian-born American comic book writer and artist, known for his work at Marvel Comics and his collaborations with Stan Lee. Worked on the creation of such characters as Spider-Man, Doctor Strange, Iron Man and the Hulk.
